# Data wishlist

Status: working list
Created: 2026-04-18

The central question is not just whether supported housing units increased. It is who the residents are, where they came from, and what changed in the referral/supply pipeline.

## Minimum useful dataset

Resident-level or aggregated fields needed:

| Field | Why it matters | Likely holder |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Current accommodation type | Distinguishes exempt supported HMO from ordinary HMO, hostel, social housing, TA | council Housing Benefit, provider returns |
| Provider and landlord | Links resident flow to business model and supply-side expansion | council, regulator, Companies House |
| Referral source | Shows whether demand is local homelessness, probation, prisons, hospitals, other councils, self-referral, provider outreach | providers, council, probation, commissioned services |
| Previous accommodation | Identifies street homelessness, asylum accommodation, prison, family/friends, private rental, another supported provider | providers, council assessments |
| Previous postcode or local authority | Tests whether other places were sending/dumping people into Birmingham | providers, council |
| Birthplace, nationality, or years resident in UK if lawfully held and ethically usable | Tests whether the growth is mainly recent migration, long-settled migrants, UK internal movement, or local residents | council, providers; may not be held or publishable |
| Immigration/asylum/refugee route where lawfully recorded | Tests asylum/refugee move-on contribution without guessing | council homelessness, Home Office, refugee support services |
| Local connection status | Separates Birmingham local demand from imported/regional/national demand | council homelessness/local connection checks |
| Main support need | Tests whether people need intensive support or mainly accommodation | providers, council assessments |
| Support hours actually delivered | Tests low-support/high-rent concern | provider records, regulator inspections |
| Criminal justice pathway | Tests prison/probation pipeline contribution | probation, prison resettlement, providers |
| Move-in and move-out dates | Distinguishes rising inflow from blocked move-on/longer stays | providers, council |
| Housing Benefit amount and eligible service charges | Connects resident pipeline to financial incentives | council HB records |

## Public data to look for first

- Birmingham supported housing strategy evidence tables.
- Birmingham Housing Benefit caseload data for specified/exempt accommodation.
- DLUHC supported housing statistics and pilot/evaluation reports.
- Parliamentary written evidence on exempt accommodation.
- NAO supported housing investigation.
- Local authority scrutiny reports for Birmingham, Blackpool, Hull, Bristol, Nottingham, and other known exempt-accommodation hotspots.
- Rough sleeping, homelessness and temporary accommodation statistics by local authority.
- Asylum support/dispersal and move-on homelessness statistics.
- Probation/prison release accommodation statistics.
- Companies House provider/landlord network data.

## FOI candidates

Ask Birmingham City Council for annual counts from 2014 onward:

- exempt/specified accommodation Housing Benefit claimants by provider;
- claimant previous local authority or previous postcode prefix if held;
- referral source categories if held;
- main support-need categories if held;
- commissioned vs non-commissioned supported accommodation;
- average eligible rent/service-charge amounts;
- counts with local connection to Birmingham;
- counts by previous local authority or previous postcode prefix;
- counts by birthplace/nationality/immigration status only if held lawfully and releasable in aggregate;
- counts of move-ins from asylum accommodation, prison/probation, hospitals, and other councils if held.

Ask West Midlands probation/prison resettlement bodies:

- annual releases or placements into Birmingham supported accommodation;
- provider names used for placements;
- out-of-area releases placed into Birmingham.

Ask sending local authorities named in Birmingham/provider evidence:

- why residents were placed in Birmingham rather than housed locally;
- whether Birmingham placement was cheaper, faster, or the only available route;
- whether placements were commissioned, spot-purchased, or informal referrals.

Ask refugee/asylum support organisations and Home Office where possible:

- asylum accommodation exits into homelessness or supported accommodation in Birmingham;
- post-decision move-on outcomes by local authority.
