## One Market, Two Forces

Supported-housing growth is best understood as two forces running together:

- Real demand from people with genuine housing and support needs.
- Strong financial incentives created by exempt-accommodation payment rules.

The first post focuses on incentive mechanics. The second focuses on Birmingham's scale gap and referral-routing evidence.

## Why This Matters for London Planning Data

Birmingham is the clearest case study, but London has the same pressure shape:

- high temporary-accommodation pressure,
- weak subsidy fit versus current costs,
- conversion activity that can be financially attractive when classified as supported/exempt.

In planning data, this can look like repeated small HMO/supported conversions by connected operators rather than purely commissioning-led growth.

## How Planistan Tracks It

Planistan's immediate focus is evidence, not slogans:

- link applications across boroughs by operator/agent,
- separate likely ordinary HMOs from likely supported/exempt HMOs,
- connect planning patterns to referral and outcomes evidence as it becomes available.

## Current Evidence Limits

We still lack routine, public, property-level data for:

- referral source,
- prior local connection,
- support hours delivered,
- move-on outcomes.

Without those fields, policy claims should stay bounded to what the cited records actually show.
